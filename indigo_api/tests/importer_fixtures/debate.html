Pan-African Parliament Hansard Report, Fourth Ordinary Session Of The 5th Parliament

May 2021



REPORT OF THE PAN-AFRICAN PARLIAMENT

Fourth Session of the Fifth Parliament

_________


Monday, 24th May, 2021


The House met at 09.15 am


[The Acting President in the Chair]


___________



HON. CHIEF FORTUNE CHARUMBIRA [THE ACTING PRESIDENT OF THE PAN- AFRICAN PARLIAMENT]: I will now allow an opportunity for silent prayer and meditation. While standing, can we listen to the National Anthem of the African Union (AU).


National Anthem of the AU played


THE ACTING PRESIDENT: Thank you, and please be seated.


May I recognise and welcome all the Honourable Members of Parliament led by the Bureau, Members from all the five regions of the continent - that includes myself. I recognise their Excellencies the




Ambassadors present here. I recognise other dignitaries who may be seated outside the Chamber and will be invited in at an appropriate time.


I will give a few remarks in opening this session. Then, I will ask the Clerk to make an announcement in respect of the swearing-in of the new Members, who will then administer the oath. Thereafter, we will have some solidarity messages. Once that is over, we will take a break to allow for the settling in and preparations to receive the Guest of Honour, whom you now know, the President of Ghana, who shall join us at about 11:00 o’clock. We will announce the rest of the proceedings after the Guest of Honour has arrived. In opening this session, I would make another speech when the Guest of Honour is here, which is more thorough. For now, let me say this is great day, and a great date the 24th May 2021.


The Pan-African Parliament once more is now back to business; back in operation, after some minimal business at committee level for more than a year - because of the problems of the pandemic of Covid-19. The delay caused the numbers of new Members to swell, and we have a very large number to

swear in today and I should say before we swear-in anyone, the numbers are such that, we may not be able to do all before the Guest of Honour arrives. This means, we may have to continue after the Guest of Honour has left. But the good news is that those Members who had gone for a year without being sworn in, today is your day; you will be sworn in.


And in this session, as you all know, we need to achieve the issue of swearing-in and of course the elections later in the week. I will not take much of the time because we have a long list of Members to swear-in. I would, therefore, call upon the Clerk to proceed in terms of the provisions of our rules.


THE CLERK: Honourable Members, I have two announcements to make. The first announcement, in accordance with Rule 16 (10) which stipulates that the term of office of the Bureau of the Pan-African Parliament shall be three years, I shall now declare vacancies before the House and inform the House that the general election of the Bureau of the Pan-African Parliament will take place on Thursday, 27 May at 2 o’clock in the afternoon, in the Idris Ndele Moussa Plenary Hall.



The elections will take place in respect of Article 12.2 of the Protocol and Rule 15 of the Pan-African Parliament’s Rules of Procedure. The elections of the Bureaus of Regional Caucuses will be held on Monday, 31 May, 2021 from 9 o’clock to 12:30. The elections of the Bureaus of the Committees will be held from Monday, 31 May, 2021 from 2 o’clock in the afternoon to 6 o’clock in the evening, and also on Tuesday, 1 June 2021 from 9 o’clock. The elections of the Bureaus of Women Caucus and Youth Caucus will be held on Wednesday, 2 June 2021 from 9 o’clock in the morning. The detailed arrangements for these meetings will be communicated in due course.


My second announcement, Mr President, Honourable Members, is to request that you bear with us for some changes in the way that your presence within the precincts of the Pan-African Parliament and your conduct within the Chamber may have been adjusted due to the need to comply with the requirements for mitigating infection because of the Covid-19 pandemic that is ongoing.


The permission that we have from the Government of the Republic of South Africa for hosting this plenary was given on a pledge


that we will strictly adhere to the Covid-19 mitigating circumstances that have been put in place by the Government of South Africa. And so, please do not be upset if you hear that the offices that you normally operate from are closed and not accessible, do not be upset if you will be required to come in this Chamber to attend your meetings and then leave immediately.


The other conditions under which we are holding this meeting in compliance with the rules for mitigating infections of the Covid-19 have been communicated to all the Honourable Members.


Thank you, Mr President.


HON. ABDIAZIZ ABDILAHI MOHAMED [SOMALIA]: On a point of order. Thank you, Mr President, for giving me the Floor. I have taken this point of order with regard to the announcement that has just been made by the Clerk.


Mr President, you and I are some of the most experienced Members in this Chamber and there may be also many others like us. Traditionally, on the first day of the session,



we used to have the elections of the chairpersons of caucuses, including important caucuses such as women and youth caucuses. So, I am wondering why we cannot keep our tradition, and have elections of chairpersons on the first day of this session, as we normally used to do. That is my question.


Thank you very much.


THE ACTING PRESIDENT: I know Madam Josiane Wawa Dahab, Senior Clerk can easily respond to that question.


THE CLERK: Mr President, Honorable Members, the rules - your rules - do not specify the order in which the elections must be conducted; that is, whether it is the Committees first or the Caucus or the Bureau. In the past, we have actually elected the Bureau first and then the Caucuses later. So that is the practice that we as Secretariat are familiar with and that is the basis on which we made the recommendation that we start with the election of the President and the Vice- Presidents and then we proceed with the Caucuses and the Committees.



However, it is really your decision, Honorable Members, because it is a matter, which is not provided for specifically in the

rules. But we would like to bear in mind that for the elections of the Bureau, they have been fixed in terms of Rule (15), sub-rule (2). It has been fixed for Thursday 27th May 2021 at 2:00 p.m. If you want to switch the order of the other elections, it would really be at your discretion.


HON. ABDIAZIZ ABDILAHI MOHAMED [SOMALIA]: Thank you for giving me the Floor again. Mr. President, I was elected as the chairperson of a caucus twice. And that happened on the first day of the session. I do not remember when that, did not happen. Mr. President, we have to keep our tradition here.


The elections of - you know - chairpersons of Bureaus of Caucuses are the second ranked next to the Bureau of the Pan-African Parliament. How can we have the election of Bureaus at the end of our session? It is impossible. Who is going to chair the caucuses now? Why are we paralyzing our institutions and activities?


Thank you very much, Mr. President.
